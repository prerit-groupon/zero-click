# Groupon Processes

> This document captures Groupon's operating principles, strategic framework, execution methodology,
> and how work is planned and delivered across the company.

---

## Mission

> To be the trusted destination for discovering high-quality local services & experiences at unbeatable value.

---

## Operating Principles

Five principles define how Groupon behaves, decides, and executes. These apply to every team,
every initiative, and every individual across the organisation.

---

### 1. Extreme Ownership

**One owner per outcome; no passengers, no excuses.**

Extreme Ownership means every important outcome has one accountable owner: not a committee,
not a shared bucket, and never "the team." This principle eliminates ambiguity, accelerates
execution, and ensures that what matters most has clear leadership.

| Dimension | Behaviour |
|-----------|-----------|
| Execution Discipline | End-to-end delivery; no task without a clear owner |
| Decision Quality | Clarity in scope, accountability, and trade-offs |
| Cultural Behavior | High trust, direct accountability across peers |

---

### 2. Speed Over Comfort

**Ship fast, learn faster. Perfect is too slow.**

Speed Over Comfort means we value progress over perfection. We optimise for learning speed,
iteration, and momentum — because fast cycles beat long debates and slow execution every time.

| Dimension | Behaviour |
|-----------|-----------|
| Execution Discipline | Bias for action and iteration speed |
| Decision Quality | Faster decisions with adequate data; adjust rapidly |
| Cultural Behavior | Celebrate progress and learning, not endless debate |

---

### 3. Impact Obsessed

**Focus only on what moves the metrics that matter.**

Impact Obsession anchors all work to measurable outcomes. It ensures the organisation's time,
talent, and resources flow toward what truly drives our business — not towards noise, effort,
or activity.

| Dimension | Behaviour |
|-----------|-----------|
| Execution Discipline | Work tied to measurable customer or merchant outcomes |
| Decision Quality | Prioritize actions that drive growth or profitability |
| Cultural Behavior | Data-driven; outcome-focused over effort-focused |

---

### 4. Simplify to Scale

**Complexity Kills, Simplicity Scales.**

Simplicity is a force multiplier. Complexity slows execution, confuses teams, and prevents the
organisation from moving at scale. Simplify to Scale means designing processes, communication,
and systems that grow cleanly and efficiently.

| Dimension | Behaviour |
|-----------|-----------|
| Execution Discipline | Design lean processes and systems |
| Decision Quality | Choose the simplest path that achieves 80% of value |
| Cultural Behavior | Promote clarity, not chaos; reduce layers and waste |

---

### 5. Disciplined

**Do more with less; constraints breed creativity.**

Discipline is how we ensure quality, consistency, and sustainable growth. It means using
resources wisely, operating leanly, and driving measurable return on everything we invest
time or money in.

| Dimension | Behaviour |
|-----------|-----------|
| Execution Discipline | Efficient use of time, capital, and technology |
| Decision Quality | Invest only in ideas with clear ROI and leverage |
| Cultural Behavior | Pride in resourcefulness; smart spending as innovation |

## Strategic Framework

### The Vision

We are entering the next phase of our transformation. The major constraints to continued
success are internal — we control our destiny. To keep accelerating, maintain a high growth
rate and reach our full potential — joining the ranks of world-class product-driven internet
companies like Uber, DoorDash, Airbnb, Spotify, and Shopify — we need to level up how we
solve customer pain points and deliver meaningful changes to our marketplace experience.

### Goals of the Framework

1. Improve how we plan, prioritize, and allocate our collective capacity
2. Better align efforts to solve customer and merchant problems and evolve our value proposition
3. Make the process more efficient — less time planning, more time doing, higher throughput

---

## What We Are Building — The Strategy Stack

From Beliefs to Initiatives, every level connects: guiding what we focus on, how we measure
progress, and how we deliver impact.

| Layer | Horizon | Purpose |
|-------|---------|---------|
| **Beliefs** | 3–5 years | Define how Groupon wins in local commerce — set by CEO/STeam |
| **North Star Metrics** | 2 years | Prove whether we are moving in the right direction |
| **Company Bets** | 6–18 months | Large cross-functional initiatives that prove Beliefs correct |
| **Functional Bets** | 6–18 months | Department-led initiatives that support Bets or Beliefs |
| **Team Initiatives** | 0–6 months | Scoped delivery work that cascades from Bets |

Work is **stackable, transparent, and tied directly to impact**.

---

## Beliefs

Three core beliefs define how Groupon wins in local commerce over the next 3–5 years.

**Belief #1:** Curated Quality & Trusted Value, Owned End-to-End, Wins Loyalty

**Belief #2:** Hyperlocal Supply Paired with Precision & Personalization Wins Local

**Belief #3:** The Next-Generation of Local Commerce Will Be AI-Native

---

## Planning Cadence

Strategy planning runs in **two 6-month cycles**:

- **Cycle A:** October → March
- **Cycle B:** April → September

Beliefs are set by the CEO/STeam and measured through North Stars. From there, we select a
limited set of Company Bets, cascade Functional Bets, and scope Team Initiatives.

---

## Company Bets

Company Bets are major, cross-functional initiatives that we believe will meaningfully move
our North Star metrics and prove our Company Beliefs correct.

**Key characteristics:**

| Characteristic | Description |
|----------------|-------------|
| Cross-functional | Require coordination between multiple teams, not just one function |
| Sponsored | Backed by an STeam sponsor and tracked in Asana for review and approval |
| Owned | Every Bet has a DRI (Directly Responsible Individual) accountable for outcomes |
| Time-boxed | Run on clear 6–12 month timelines with defined success criteria |
| Resource-backed | Priority access to people, budget, and leadership attention |
| Measurable | Must directly connect to North Star metric improvement |
| Limited in number | Only 3–8 Company Bets at a time to ensure focus and impact |

> See [`bets/company/`](bets/company/) for the full list of current Company Bets.

---

## Functional Bets

Functional Bets are significant projects generally driven by a single department, but they
require support and resources from other departments to succeed. They often support Company
Bets but can also exist independently to address function-specific or market-specific needs
that support our Beliefs.

> See [`bets/functional/`](bets/functional/) for the full list of current Functional Bets.

---

## GROW² — How We Execute

**Goal, Results, Owners, Work Plan**

GROW² is how we work at Groupon. Every project, big or small, starts with Goal, Results,
and Owner. GROW² ensures every project is tied to business value, delivered in focused
slices, and judged on evidence — so we reduce waste, align teams, and stay impact-driven.

All work is executed through the GROW² framework — short, time-boxed, measurable runs that
keep us focused and accountable.

For full guidelines, examples, and templates refer to **GRPN GROW Framework v2.0**.
